HR Policy Handbook
Version: 1.1
Document Type: Internal Policy Reference

Overview
This handbook summarizes selected company policies for employee work arrangements, paid leave, and security requirements. It is intended to provide a simple internal reference for common policy questions.

Remote Work Policy
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week with manager approval.

Remote Work Clarifications
The maximum remote work allowance is 3 days each week, provided the employee's manager approves the arrangement.
Employees are permitted to work from home no more than 3 days in a standard workweek unless a separate written exception is granted.
With manager approval, employees can work off-site, from home, or remotely for as many as 3 days per week.
The remote work limit is 3 approved work-from-home days in a week.

Paid Time Off Policy
Full-time employees receive 20 days of paid time off per calendar year.

Paid Time Off Clarifications
The annual PTO allocation for full-time employees is 20 days each calendar year.
Full-time employees are granted 20 paid leave days per year.
The standard yearly vacation and paid time off benefit for full-time staff is 20 days.
Employees in full-time roles receive 20 PTO days annually.

PTO Carryover Policy
Unused paid time off does not carry over into the next calendar year unless an exception is approved in writing by Human Resources.

PTO Carryover Clarifications
The standard policy is that unused PTO expires at the end of the calendar year and is not rolled over automatically.
PTO carryover is not allowed by default.
Employees should not expect unused vacation or PTO days to roll into the following year unless HR approves a written exception.
Any PTO rollover requires written HR approval.
The default carryover policy is no carryover unless approved in writing by Human Resources.

Security Requirements
Employees must complete annual security awareness training and use multi-factor authentication for all company systems.

Security Clarifications
All employees are required to complete security awareness training every year.
Annual security training is mandatory for employees.
Employees must use multi-factor authentication, also called MFA, when accessing company systems.
All company systems require multi-factor authentication for employee access.
